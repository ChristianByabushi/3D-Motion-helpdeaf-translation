## Overview

Building a cloud-based system that converts text and speech into real-time 3D sign language animations using AI and motion synthesis.

Building an AI-powered platform that translates written and spoken language into accurate, real-time 3D sign language animations used by Deaf people in education, healthcare, and public services. This internship will focus on building and scaling the core technical infrastructure that powers this system. Interns will work across software engineering, machine learning, and 3D animation pipelines to transform research and motion capture data into production-ready web systems.

Interns will be responsible for:
- Designing and implementing APIs that serve sign language translation and animation data
- Building real-time streaming pipelines (e.g., WebSockets) for delivering 3D animations to web and mobile clients
- Processing and optimizing motion capture and 3D animation data (BVH, glTF, skeletal animations) for web delivery
- Integrating machine learning model outputs (translation, pose, motion quality) into live production systems
- Improving system performance, latency, caching, and scalability on cloud platforms
- Working with 3D animators, ML engineers, and Deaf sign language users to validate technical and linguistic accuracyB

Interns will be embedded in a highly interdisciplinary team combining AI research, animation, and accessibility technology. The work will directly support Deaf users who rely on HelpDeaf to access digital content and services. This is not a research-only internship ‚ÄĒ interns will contribute to production systems used by real users.

## Research Statement

### Multi-modal Frontiers

My next phase of research expands into Multi-modal Generative AI by developing a cloud-based system to bridge the gap in Deaf education and healthcare. The system translates spoken African languages into real-time 3D sign language animations ‚ÄĒ a task that transcends simple mapping. It necessitates a sophisticated encoder-decoder framework capable of projecting phonological speech embeddings into high-dimensional spatial-temporal motion vectors.

The pipeline integrates three core AI components:
1. **Automatic Speech Recognition (ASR)** ‚ÄĒ transcribing spoken input from target African languages
2. **Neural Machine Translation (NMT)** ‚ÄĒ mapping transcribed text to sign language glosses
3. **Motion Synthesis AI** ‚ÄĒ generating fluid, anatomically accurate 3D sign language animations from motion vectors

### Target Languages

The system will initially support the **5 most spoken languages in Africa**:

| # | Language | Primary Region | Approx. Speakers |
|---|----------|---------------|-----------------|
| 1 | **Arabic** | North Africa, Horn of Africa | ~300 million |
| 2 | **Swahili (Kiswahili)** | East & Central Africa | ~200 million |
| 3 | **Hausa** | West Africa (Nigeria, Niger, Ghana) | ~150 million |
| 4 | **Yoruba** | West Africa (Nigeria, Benin, Togo) | ~50 million |
| 5 | **Oromo** | Horn of Africa (Ethiopia, Kenya) | ~40 million |

### Vision for Impact

By integrating ASR, NMT, and motion-synthesis AI, my goal is to dismantle the barriers of digital exclusion. I aim to contribute to the Vector Institute's mission by developing AI that is not only computationally efficient but socially transformative. My objective is to ensure that the next 400 million digital citizens in Africa have equitable access to technology, shifting the paradigm toward AI that is built by and for the global majority.
